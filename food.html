Famous Foods of Uttar Pradesh

Samosa

  • Golden fried snack filled with spicy potato mixture.
  • Served hot with chutneys across every town and village.
  • Common evening snack and breakfast item in UP.
  • Often paired with tea or lassi.
  • Sold at every street corner and halwai shop.

Jalebi

  • Sweet spiral made of fermented batter, deep fried and dipped in sugar syrup.
  • Crispy outside and juicy inside ‚Äì a classic UP sweet.
  • Often enjoyed with milk or curd in winter mornings.
  • Prepared fresh in ghee in local shops.
  • A must-have during festivals and weddings.

Kachori

  • Deep-fried puffed pastry filled with spicy urad dal mixture.
  • Served with tangy potato curry (aloo sabzi).
  • Popular breakfast dish across UP towns.
  • Especially loved in Banaras, Kanpur, and Agra.
  • Often enjoyed with jalebi or curd.

Dahi Bhalle

  • A unique food famous in the various regions of UP.
  • served with spicy and sweet curd bhallas.
  • Combines spicy, tangy, and cool flavors in one plate.
  • Usually eaten during breakfast and fairs.
  • Rare and authentic flavor experience from rural U.P.

Banarasi Paan

  • Iconic sweet paan prepared using betel leaf, gulkand, and dry fruits.
  • Symbol of hospitality and culture in Varanasi.
  • Known for its freshness and aromatic flavor.
  • Available in traditional and modern flavors like chocolate paan.
  • Famous across India for its name and taste.
