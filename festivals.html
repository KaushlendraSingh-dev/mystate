Festivals of Uttar Pradesh

Kumbh Mela

  • Hosted in Prayagraj, this is the largest religious gathering in the world.
  • Millions bathe at the Triveni Sangam to cleanse sins and seek blessings.
  • Features processions of saints, ashram rituals, and spiritual discourse.
  • Occurs once every 12 years, alternating with Ardh Kumbh every 6 years.
  • Recognized as an intangible cultural heritage by UNESCO.

Diwali

  • Celebrated with grandeur in Ayodhya, believed to be Lord Ram’s return day.
  • Entire city lights up with earthen lamps, breaking world records every year.
  • Fireworks, rangolis, sweets, and prayers bring joy across the state.
  • Homes and temples are cleaned, decorated, and filled with prosperity vibes.
  • Celebration includes Govardhan Puja and Bhai Dooj over five days.

Holi

  • Known as the festival of colors, celebrated especially in Barsana and Mathura.
  • Barsana's Lathmar Holi features playful stick fights between men and women.
  • Symbolizes the victory of good over evil through the Holika Dahan bonfire.
  • People smear colors, sing, and dance across towns and temples.
  • Offers a deep social and spiritual connection among all communities.

Ram Navami

  • Commemorates the birth of Lord Ram, celebrated in full spirit at Ayodhya.
  • Processions of Ram Lalla idols are taken out through decorated streets.
  • Ramayana path, bhajans, and havans are performed in homes and temples.
  • Devotees fast and offer prasad in devotion to Maryada Purushottam Ram.
  • The city hosts lakhs of pilgrims in this sacred annual festival.

Krishna Janmashtami

  • Birth anniversary of Lord Krishna, celebrated with energy in Mathura & Vrindavan.
  • Midnight puja marks Krishna’s birth moment, with devotional chants and songs.
  • Jhankis (tableaus), plays, and Krishna Leela enactments are organized.
  • Dahi Handi competitions add fun and youth energy to the event.
  • Children often dress up as Krishna and Radha during the celebration.
